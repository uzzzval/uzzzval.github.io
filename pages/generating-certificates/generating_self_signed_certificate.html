How to generate a self-signed certificate on Windows 10 using Powershell

1. Click on the search option at the taskbar, and type Powershell, when 'Windows Powershell' appears in the Best Match section, right-click on it and then left-click 'Run as administrator'.




2. On the Powershell Window, type the command as shown in the screenshot and press enter.




3. When the certificate is successfully generated, you will see the following details:




4. Click on the search option in the taskbar and enter 'mmc'. When 'mmc' appears in the Best Match Section, click on it.




5. In the Console1 window, initially, you will not be able to see anything. Click on 'File', then 'Add/Remove Snap-in..'.




6. You will now observe a 'Add or Remove Snap-ins' window, click on Certificates and then click on the 'Add' option.




7. In the Certificates snap-in window, select Computer account and then click Next.




8. On the next Select Computer window, select the option Local computer, and then click Finish.




9. Now, you will observe Certificates(Local Computer) tree appearing below the Console Root in the Console1 window(as shown below). When you will expand the Certificates , then Personal, then Certificates option, you will observe the certificate which we created in Step 2.




10. Your Self-Signed Certificate is ready for use now.
